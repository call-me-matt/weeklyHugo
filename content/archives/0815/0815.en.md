## OpenStreetMap Foundation

- The OpenStreetMap Foundation Board has formally [submitted](https://en.osm.town/@openstreetmap/116165330892920654) comments on a proposal by the Open Geospatial Consortium to standardise the Overture Maps Foundation’s Global Entity Reference System. In its response, the Foundation clarified that it does not oppose the concept of a globally interoperable geographic identifier system. However, it urged the OGC to recognise that geographic reality cannot be reduced to a single authoritative source. The board argued that geographic knowledge is generated not only within the data centres of major technology firms, but also through the distributed efforts of volunteers mapping streets, neighbourhoods, and communities around the world. Any standard seeking OGC endorsement, the Foundation said, should be inclusive enough to accommodate both centralised and community-driven sources of geographic data.

## Maps

- OpenStreetMap Americana has made several more multilingual [improvements](https://community.openstreetmap.org/t/osm-americana-your-local-language-companion/141757) to support language dialects and apply dual language labels to more places. Web developers can [install](https://github.com/osm-americana/diplomat/) Diplomat to give any MapLibre map the same localised labels as OSM Americana.

## Software

- GanderPL has [built](https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/GanderPL/diary/408286) an OSM tagging [MCP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Model_Context_Protocol) server [using](https://github.com/openstreetmap/id-tagging-schema) the iD Tagging Schema.

- Conveyal has [developed](https://medium.com/conveyal-blog/introducing-osmix-365c4b4332ef) Osmix, a collection of [composable](https://github.com/conveyal/osmix) libraries for reading, querying, merging, and transforming OpenStreetMap PBF data in browsers or Node\.js.

- Sarath Sabarish has [published](https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/sarath%20sabarish/diary/408305) a diary entry explaining how SafeStreets, a free walkability scoring tool, uses OSM data, Nominatim for geocoding, Overpass API for pedestrian infrastructure and 15-minute city scoring. The post includes a concrete Chiang Mai example showing how missing `highway=crossing` tags produce near-zero crossing scores, and calls on SE Asia mappers to improve pavement (sidewalk) and crossing tagging.

## Programming

- Pascal Neis [described](https://neis-one.org/2026/03/flappy-birds-coding-assistants) his experience of using LLM AI programming applications. He tested several AI coding assistants to automate coding tasks, building tasks iteratively in a local environment 'from Flappy Birds to WebGIS'. He compared Microsoft Copilot, OpenAI Codex, and Anthropic Claude. He is seriously considering trying this kind of setup with his students next semester.

- HeiGIT [explained](https://heigit.org/how-street-level-imagery-and-deep-learning-are-helping-map-global-infrastructure) how street-level imagery combined with deep learning methods is transforming how we detect and map critical infrastructure characteristics, which are often missing from existing datasets. Applications range from road surface classification and waste detection to pavement width measurement and weather-adaptive routing.

## OSM in the media

- At FOSDEM 2026 Petya Kangalova, Senior Tech Partnerships Manager at HOT, [spoke](https://lwn.net/Articles/1057691/) about how this humanitarian NGO has built a comprehensive technology stack based on the OpenStreetMap environment, designed to support local communities in mapping their surroundings, to strengthen disaster response efforts and support humanitarian operations worldwide.

The one-hour meeting will occur on [March 11, 2026, 11:00 ET (UTC-4)](https://www.timeanddate.com/worldclock/fixedtime.html?iso=20260311T1500&msg=Event). You can [register](https://nasalifelines.org/community-connect-sign-up/) for free.

- Chromy [showcased](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47205637) [Flexport Atlas](https://atlas.flexport.com/), an interactive global map partially powered by OpenStreetMap data that tracks cargo vessels, ports, and aircraft with live data updated every two hours, including precise vessel statuses (moored, at anchor, or in transit), and port dwell times.
