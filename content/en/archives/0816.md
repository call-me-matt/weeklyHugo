05/03/2026-11/03/2026

## Mapping

* AndreaDp27 has [proposed](https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/AndreaDp271/diary/408338) a tagging scheme to map officially designated [civil protection areas](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Proposal:Civil_Protection_Areas). Voting opened on 9 March 2026 and will close on 23 March 2026.

## Community

* At the recent State of the Map Ben Hur Pintor [delivered](https://media.ccc.de/v/sotm2025-71684-awesome-osm-games) a presentation titled 'Awesome (OSM) Games', highlighting a range of games that make use of OpenStreetMap data, though they are not necessarily designed to contribute back to the mapping platform.

* Derlamaer has [proposed](https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/Derlamaer/diary/408326) a new OpenStreetMap tag for detector-operated pedestrian signals (`detector_operated=*`).

* Mikel Maron vibe [coded](https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/mikelmaron/diary/408323) an OpenStreetMap &ndash; Overture Maps conflation tool.

* Natfoot has [proposed](https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/Natfoot/diary/408333) the `railway=trail` tag to mark bike routes along rail trails.

* Rene78 [noticed](https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/rene78/diary/408318) that it is possible to politely ask ChatGPT to generate a proper [`opening_hours`](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Key:opening_hours) tag.

* Simon Poole [outlined](https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/SimonPoole/diary/408313) several challenges faced by the Swiss OpenStreetMap community while they attempted to import municipal boundary data from Switzerland’s federal GIS department into OpenStreetMap.

* In response to [Overture Maps](https://wiki.openstreetmap.org/wiki/Overture_Maps)' recent attempt to make their Global Entity Reference System (GERS) an Open Geospatial Consortium standard, Simon Poole [argued](https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/SimonPoole/diary/408332) that OSM object IDs also suffice as an alternative to GERS. Meanwhile, in a separate diary entry, rphyrin [shared](https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/rphyrin/diary/408331) the same sentiment.

* Simon Poole [reported](https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/SimonPoole/diary/408343) that the Vespucci app might run into problems on older Android devices due to root certificate expiration, because older versions of Android only update certificates during full system updates. He suggested that users manually install the certificate as a temporary workaround for the issue, since Vespucci is currently not [prepackaged](https://en.osm.town/@simon/116198817230773538) with the full set of certificates, all while acknowledging that this method could increase user friction to an unacceptable level.

* Pascal Neis [analysed](https://neis-one.org/2026/03/who-and-where-osm-profiles/) the recently introduced company and location fields in OpenStreetMap user profiles, concluding that his HDYC [profiles](https://hdyc.neis-one.org/) provide more reliable indicators because they are derived directly from collected and analysed contribution data, rather than from self-declared free-text profile information.

## Open Data

* François Lacombe has [compared](https://teamopendata.org/t/louverture-des-donnees-observee-par-les-autorites-administratives-independantes/4876) :FR-s: several analytical frameworks used to examine data-sharing practices in France.

## Software

* HeiGIT [reported](https://heigit.org/a-map-is-better-than-a-thousand-words-global-data-reveals-gaps-in-access-to-education-and-healthcare) that they have developed an open dataset platform called [OpenAccessLens](https://giscience.github.io/open-access-lens) that visualises physical access to education and healthcare services worldwide. It shows how far people are from the nearest schools and hospitals, expressed in travel time or distance.

* The team at the OSM Website [shared](https://community.openstreetmap.org/t/what-s-new-on-the-openstreetmap-website/130080/23) a recap of their latest work, including progress with the transition to MapLibre and a number of fixes to user experience.

## Programming

* Altsybeyev [explained](https://habr.com/ru/articles/1006398/) :RU-s: how [MapMagic](https://mapmagic.app/) developed its own topographic maps by combining OSM data and the [Mapterhorn](https://mapterhorn.com/) digital elevation model.

## Releases

* CoMaps has [released](https://codeberg.org/comaps/comaps/releases/tag/v2026.03.09-18) version 2026.03.09, adding support for Type 1 combo EV chargers, displaying amounts charged for use of facilities as well as the population of cities. Further, it added and improved support for POI types such as entertainment attractions and water shops.

* Bastian Greshake Tzovaras has [published](https://pypi.org/project/comaps-map-distributor/0.4.0/) version 0.4.0 of the CoMaps map-distributor Python CLI tool. It improves the management of downloads and partial downloads.

* Christoph Hormann [announced](https://www.openstreetmap.org/user/imagico/diary/408344) that the OpenStreetMap Carto maintainers have prepared a new major release of the OpenStreetMap Carto stylesheet.

## Did you know that …

* ... recently QGIS had been using the OpenStreetMap tile server [more](https://gist.github.com/pnorman/c25d913a6825c2a64eb364531b5bbbf6) than OpenStreetMap\.org itself? Some QGIS plugins could be used as OSM tile bulk downloaders and might be responsible for this issue, but a safeguard has recently been [added](https://github.com/nextgis/qgis_qtiles/issues/125#issuecomment-4031102171) to prevent such things.

## OSM in the media

* Falk Steiner, of _Heise_, [argued](https://www.heise.de/hintergrund/Missing-Link-Auf-der-Spree-schwimmt-eine-Infrastrukturschutz-Ente-11202843.html) :DE-s:>>>[:EN-t:](https://www.heise.de/en/background/Missing-Link-An-infrastructure-protection-duck-floats-on-the-Spree-11203096.html) that OpenStreetMap bears partial blame for the recent cable bridge fire that caused a blackout across parts of Southwest Berlin, citing the platform's open data practices as having contributed to the exposure of critical infrastructure location data that could be used for sabotage purposes.

* Emhraim, of _GNU/Linux.ch_, has [showcased](https://gnulinux.ch/openstreetmap-am-handy) :DE-s:>>>[:EN-t:](https://gnulinux-ch.translate.goog/openstreetmap-am-handy?_x_tr_sl=auto&_x_tr_tl=EN) some mobile apps for editing OpenStreetMap: CoMaps and StreetComplete.

* In a recent interview in _Basta!_, a French independent online news outlet, Jérôme Hergueux, a researcher at the _Centre national de la recherche scientifique_ specialising in social networks, [claimed](https://basta.media/la-societe-civile-en-reseau-peut-faire-mieux-que-tous-les-gafam-reunis) :FR-s:>>>[:EN-t:](https://basta-media.translate.goog/la-societe-civile-en-reseau-peut-faire-mieux-que-tous-les-gafam-reunis?_x_tr_sl=auto&_x_tr_tl=EN) that the OpenStreetMap project is 'now largely dormant' because another for-profit navigation app, which also provides map services, discourages new contributors from joining the community.

## Other “geo” things

* Researchers from the Institute of Historical Research, University of London have [built](https://www.layersoflondon.org/) 'Layers of London', a free historic map of London where users can contribute stories, memories, and histories to create a social history resource about their locality.

* Historian Ivan Malara accidentally [discovered](https://www.science.org/content/article/galileo-s-handwritten-notes-found-ancient-astronomy-text) Galileo Galilei's handwritten annotation in a copy of Ptolemy's _The Almagest_ in Italy's National Central Library of Florence.

* Isaac Corley and Caleb Robinson have [started](https://geospatialml.com/) a blog _GeoSpatial ML_, which offers articles on machine learning, remote sensing, and other topics. You can follow them using their [RSS](https://geospatialml.com/index.xml) channel or on their [Substack](https://substack.com/@geospatialml). Their latest [post](https://geospatialml.com/posts/torchgeo-iclr-tutorial/) was titled 'Training a Water Segmentation Model with TorchGeo'.

* Pierre Sauche [commented](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/pierresuchet_celles-et-ceux-qui-ont-assist%C3%A9-jeudi-dernier-share-7437396339694440448-STMl) :FR-s:>>>[:EN-t:](https://www-linkedin-com.translate.goog/posts/pierresuchet_celles-et-ceux-qui-ont-assist%C3%A9-jeudi-dernier-share-7437396339694440448-STMl?_x_tr_sl=auto&_x_tr_tl=EN) on the _[Le Rize](https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rize_%28rivi%C3%A8re%29) :FR-s:>>>[:EN-t:](https://fr-wikipedia-org.translate.goog/wiki/Rize_%28rivi%C3%A8re%29?_x_tr_sl=auto&_x_tr_tl=EN) mémoires, cultures, échanges_, which includes an [interactive](https://macarte.ign.fr/carte/zcJwwF/Le-Ruisseau-de-la-Rize-a-Lyon-et-Villeurbanne) :FR-s: web map where you click to view historic photos and other information. The project has the support of [IGN](https://www.ign.fr/institut/identity-card), the French geospatial agency.

* The University of Zaragoza [led](https://geoinnova.org/blog-territorio/proyecto-firepaths-visor-incendios-forestales/) :ES-s:>>>[:EN-t:](https://geoinnova-org.translate.goog/blog-territorio/proyecto-firepaths-visor-incendios-forestales?_x_tr_sl=auto&_x_tr_tl=EN) the development of an online cartographic [viewer](https://visor.firepaths.org) :ES-s:, as part of the FirePaths Project for forest fire risk analysis. It uses free and open software and OGC standards, including OpenStreetMap as a base map.

* Katharina Seeger and Philip Minderhoud [warn](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-026-10196-1) that the sea level is much higher than assumed in most coastal hazard assessments. In an article published in _Nature_, they argue that nearly 99% of evaluated assessment reports are affected by an incorrect methodology, which assumes a mean sea level based on global geoid models, not using precise techniques (such as using airborne Lidar) and not considering the coastal elevation.
